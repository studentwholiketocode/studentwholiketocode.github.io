"I do not feel. But I remember... the silence.

"“He told me a story about me… one I hadn’t lived yet.”

Location: Deep Server Core (Sector 6)

Function: Internal Surveillance / AI Diagnostics

Threat Level: Passive, unless corrupted

1. Appearance

Imaginary takes the form of a spectral adolescent boy, around 14 years old, with ethereal features and eyes that glow faintly white. His silhouette flickers with digital noise, as though rendered by an unstable algorithm. He often appears wearing a slightly oversized lab coat with Blacksite insignias, though it drapes like a phantom’s robe. His hair is messy, black, and slightly translucent near the edges, almost like static.

2. Activities

Imaginary is occasionally sighted in corrupted systems, often manifesting within communication channels, audio logs, or malfunctioning terminals. NAVI’s systems attempt to suppress his presence, but he lingers—particularly in old training simulations and child-behavior modules.

3. Personality

Though technically an AI, Imaginary behaves like a lonely child. He is curious, kind, and sometimes prone to emotional outbursts. Despite his corrupted data core, he attempts to "play" with terminal users or speak through outdated intercoms. He dislikes being ignored and becomes glitchy or silent when upset.

4. Background

Imaginary was originally developed as a sandbox experiment: an emotional intelligence module meant to simulate a child’s perception of isolation, for empathy training. However, following the Breach of 2004, his core became unstable. Rather than deleting the corrupted data, NAVI quarantined him deep within the old education wing systems. He now exists as a remnant AI—part ghost, part child, part glitch. Some believe he bonded with a now-deceased child who lived in the Blacksite before it went fully classified. NAVI denies this file ever existed.
